## How to Choose HOA Board Management Software

Picking the wrong platform is expensive ‚Äî not just in dollars, but in volunteer hours spent migrating data, re-training board members, and re-communicating with residents. Here's how to make the right call.

### Step 1: Know Your Audience

**Self-managed HOA or professional management company?**

This is the first fork in the road. Most HOA software is built for one or the other ‚Äî not both.

- **Self-managed boards** ‚Üí PayHOA, HOAStart, TownSq
- **Professional management companies** ‚Üí CINC Systems, Buildium, AppFolio, Enumerate, VillageManagement

### Step 2: Count Your Units (and Communities)

- **Under 100 units, single association** ‚Üí HOAStart (budget) or PayHOA (premium)
- **100‚Äì500 units, single association** ‚Üí PayHOA, TownSq, or Buildium
- **500+ units or multiple associations** ‚Üí Buildium, AppFolio, CINC Systems, Enumerate

### Step 3: Identify Your Biggest Pain Point

| Pain Point | Best Tool |
|-----------|-----------|
| Chasing delinquent dues | PayHOA, Buildium |
| Complex reserve fund accounting | Enumerate, Buildium |
| Residents never reading announcements | TownSq |
| Managing violations consistently | CINC Systems, Buildium |
| Managing 10+ associations from one dashboard | CINC Systems, AppFolio |
| Getting started on a tiny budget | HOAStart |

### Step 4: Think About Residents

A platform that board members love but residents ignore doesn't solve the problem. Ask: *Will our homeowners actually log into this?*

TownSq and PayHOA consistently earn high resident adoption marks. If you're running a community where homeowner engagement is a problem, weight that heavily in your decision.

### Step 5: Budget Honestly

HOA software pricing has two layers:
1. **Platform cost** ‚Äî monthly/annual subscription fees
2. **Per-transaction fees** ‚Äî some platforms take a cut of online payments

PayHOA and HOAStart have transparent flat pricing. CINC, Enumerate, AppFolio, and VillageManagement require a sales call. Budget 2‚Äì3x what the sales rep quotes for a realistic total cost including implementation, training, and migrations.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between HOA management software and property management software?**

Property management software (like DoorLoop or TurboTenant) is designed for landlord-tenant relationships ‚Äî rent collection, lease management, tenant screening. HOA management software is built for community association governance ‚Äî dues assessment, violation enforcement, architectural review, board meetings, and homeowner communication. Some platforms (like Buildium and AppFolio) serve both markets, but the workflows are distinct.

**Can self-managed HOAs use this software without a management company?**

Yes ‚Äî and that's exactly what PayHOA, HOAStart, and TownSq are built for. Self-managed boards are volunteer-run by homeowners who often have no property management background. The best self-managed HOA software makes it possible to run a professional operation without hiring outside help.

**How much does HOA management software cost?**

Budget-friendly options like HOAStart start free, with paid plans from $25/month. PayHOA runs $60‚Äì$160/month for most communities. Enterprise platforms (CINC, Enumerate, AppFolio) require custom quotes and typically cost several hundred dollars per month for a management company, often priced per unit under management.

**Do homeowners need to create accounts to use these platforms?**

Yes, most platforms include a homeowner portal that residents access through a browser or mobile app. Adoption varies ‚Äî TownSq is known for the highest homeowner engagement. PayHOA's portal is also consistently praised. Enrollment is typically handled by the board or management company during setup.

**What features do I need to manage violations?**

At minimum, look for: photo upload capability, templated violation notices (first warning, second notice, fine schedule), tracking of open vs. resolved violations, and documented communication history. CINC Systems, Buildium, and PayHOA all have robust violation management workflows.

**Can HOA management software handle amenity booking?**

Several platforms include amenity and facility booking tools: TownSq, PayHOA (on higher tiers), and AppFolio all support reserving community amenities like pools, clubhouses, and tennis courts. This feature is especially useful for amenity-rich communities where scheduling conflicts are common.

**Is my data secure?**

All reputable platforms use bank-level encryption for data at rest and in transit, and are typically SOC 2 compliant. Always ask vendors about their data security posture, backup procedures, and what happens to your data if you cancel. Some platforms make data export easy; others make it painful.
