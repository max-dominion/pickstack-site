## RentRedi Overview

RentRedi launched in 2016 and has built a loyal user base among independent landlords. In 2023, it merged with TurboTenant (they now operate under the same parent), though both products continue to run independently.

### What RentRedi Does Well

**Flat-rate pricing is a genuine advantage.** RentRedi charges a flat monthly or annual fee regardless of how many units you manage. For growing portfolios, this means your costs don't scale with your doors.

**The mobile app is excellent.** RentRedi's iOS and Android apps are consistently well-reviewed. Managing maintenance requests, running tenant screening, and reviewing rent payments from your phone is seamless.

**Tenant screening via TransUnion** covers credit, criminal, and eviction history with competitive pricing.

**Online rent collection** with automated reminders, partial payments, and late fee handling is well-implemented.

**The pricing is hard to beat for landlords with many units.** A landlord managing 30 units pays the same flat rate as one managing 5 â€” at around $20/month, that math works out to under $1/unit.

### Where RentRedi Falls Short

- Accounting is basic. RentRedi tracks income and expenses but lacks full general ledger capabilities.
- Reporting is limited compared to DoorLoop.
- Owner portals are less polished â€” not ideal for professional property managers with owner-clients.
- Customer support has mixed reviews.
- DoorLoop's feature depth is meaningfully greater.

## DoorLoop Overview

DoorLoop launched in 2019 and has grown quickly, positioning itself as the modern, user-friendly alternative to legacy PM software like Buildium and AppFolio, while targeting the same market.

### What DoorLoop Does Well

**Feature depth is impressive.** DoorLoop's platform covers the full PM workflow: online listings, applications, tenant screening, digital leases, rent collection, maintenance management, owner portals, full accounting, and reporting â€” all in one well-designed interface.

**Accounting is proper GL.** DoorLoop offers a full general ledger with bank reconciliation, owner distributions, and detailed financial reporting. For property managers handling owner funds, this matters.

**Owner portals are polished.** DoorLoop's owner portal is one of the better ones in its price tier â€” useful for professional property managers presenting data to clients.

**Support quality is strong.** DoorLoop is consistently praised for responsive, knowledgeable support â€” a meaningful differentiator from RentRedi's more mixed reputation.

**The platform scales.** Whether you're managing 5 units or 500, DoorLoop's architecture accommodates growth.

### Where DoorLoop Falls Short

- Higher starting price ($69/month for up to 20 units) compared to RentRedi's flat fee.
- Per-unit pricing means costs scale with portfolio growth.
- Still a relatively young platform â€” some features less mature than Buildium or AppFolio.
- Overkill for landlords who just need basic rent collection and expense tracking.
